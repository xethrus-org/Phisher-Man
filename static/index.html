Frequently Asked Questions

What is Phishing?

  Phishing is a form of social engineering that is most often used to steal or trick a user into revealing sensitive data. Sensitive data may include information such as passwords, bank details, or identifying information such as Social Security Numbers. In a corporate setting, this could mean revealing information important to a company, such as the emails, passwords, or other sensitive information of a company's owners, employees, or even users.
  One of the most common phishing attack methods is through email, where attackers will send users fake emails to trick them into opening their fake email and clicking on a link. Once the link has been clicked, the attacker can take several new avenues of attack, such as attempting to download malware on the victim's device or continuing the phishing attack by prompting the victim for sensitive information.

What is a Phishing Simulator?

  A phishing simulator is a piece of software that simulates a real phishing attack in order to determine how vulnerable a group of people or, most commonly, a company's employees to certain types of phishing attacks. This software will send out fake phishing emails that do not contain any malicious links, but are designed to mimic the look and feel of real, malicious phishing emails in order to properly bait the receivers of the simulation and make them believe that the email is genuine.
  If a user falls for the fake phishing attack, whether it be by clicking on a certain link, entering sensitive details into the simulation, or attempting to download random software, this failure is documented by the phishing simulator software and sent to the user of the software which, in this case, would be the employer or manager of the simulation's receivers.
  The user or sender of the emails generally is able to then view a report or a list of statistics that document the success rate of the receivers, which allows the sender to determine how important phishing awareness and implementation of countermeasures to phishing and social engineering is to their group or company.
